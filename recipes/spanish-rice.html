Spanish Rice

My take on Spanish rice. Smoky, earthy, and tender without being fluffy.


Carlos O'Kelly's had my favorite take on Spanish rice. I spent years trying to find the recipe.
Everything I found online that looked close invariably disappointed me.
After numerous attempts, I finally came up with this. It's not quite the same, but close enough for me.



Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Jasmine rice
  • 1 Tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1 Tbsp tomato paste
  • 1.5 Tbsp chicken base (I use Better Than Boullion)
  • 2 Tbsp smoked SPanish paprika
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp Ancho chile powder
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1.75 cups water

Directions:

  1. Heat butter in medium saucepan on medium heat until it begins to foam.
  2. Add olive oil and rice. Stir until rice is well coated.
  3. Continue to stir until rice is fragrant and turns light brown
  4. Add the tomato paste and stir allowing it to cook for a minute or so.
  5. Reduce heat to low. Add the chicken base and continue to stir for an additional minute.
  6. Turn the heat back up to medium high and add the spices and a splash of the water. Stir until incorporated. Add the remaining water and stir well
  7. Once it's close to boiling, reduce heat to low and cover. Cook for 12 minutes or until all the water has been absorbed.
